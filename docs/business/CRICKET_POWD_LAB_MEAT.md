# Cricket Protein — Cooperative Model & Climate Connection

## What This Document Is

A comparison of cricket protein powder against lab-grown meat and traditional protein alternatives — and an argument for the cooperative, community-funded model as the right structure for a novel food company.

---

## Why Insects, Not Lab Meat

Lab-grown meat is capital-intensive, requires cold chain at scale, and is still working through regulatory approval in most markets. It is a technically impressive solution to one protein problem. But it reproduces the industrial meat model: centralized production, large capital requirement, equity structure that extracts rather than distributes.

Cricket protein sidesteps this entirely.

| Factor | Cricket protein | Lab-grown meat | Conventional animal |
|--------|----------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Land use | Minimal | Facility-dependent | High |
| Water use | Very low | Moderate | Very high (beef: 1800L/kg) |
| GHG emissions | ~1% of beef | ~50% of beef (current) | High |
| Feed conversion | ~2:1 | ~3–10:1 (current) | ~7:1 (beef) |
| Capital to start | Low | Very high | Moderate |
| Regulatory path | Clear (Canada, EU) | In progress | Clear |
| Consumer barrier | Unfamiliarity | Familiarity + disgust parity | Zero barrier |

Cricket protein doesn't need to win on taste or familiarity first. It needs to build a community that carries it into the mainstream. EBYS is designed to be that community.

## The Cooperative Structure

EBYS is not a cricket protein company that sponsors music. It's a music technology project that uses cricket protein as the economic backbone.

The difference matters for the cooperative model:

**Traditional food company:** investor capital → build supply chain → market → extract margin → return to investors.

**EBYS model:** artist and DJ community → organic brand exposure → powder sales → margin distributes back to the community (via CRKT) → community grows.

There are no external investors in the margin distribution. EBYS covers costs. Everything else flows out to CRKT holders. This is a cooperative structure: the people who create value (artists, DJs, curators) are the same people who receive the margin.

The supplier relationship is white-label at first — Entomo Farms or equivalent — to remove production risk entirely. EBYS handles brand, distribution, and community. The supplier handles production.

## The Climate Loop

The EBYS remixing engine temperature trigger:

```
entropy = clamp(0.5 + (δT / 5.0), 0.0, 1.0)
```

Where δT = today's temperature − 10-year rolling average for this date.

When the planet is hotter than historical baseline: entropy rises, composite mixes increase, curator activity is higher, tip probability is higher. More tips → more CRKT conversions → more community stake in powder revenue.

When the planet is cooler than historical baseline: entropy falls, the music is more conservative, curator income from tips is lower — but the company selling cricket protein as a climate solution is selling more of it (the message lands harder on a normal-temperature day: "this is what a sustainable diet looks like, not the emergency version").

**Either state benefits CRKT holders:**
- Hot planet: more tipping activity → more CRKT accumulation
- Cool planet (climate win): more powder sales from a credible climate narrative → more CRKT distributions

The hedge is structurally honest: EBYS doesn't profit from climate failure. It profits either way, but for different reasons in each scenario.

## The Community as Sales Force

Artists don't pitch powder. They perform. Listeners watch a EBYS set and see the spider, the brand, the name. The radio streams have the buy link. The instrument is named after eating bugs. It travels naturally.

The social experiment: people who push boundaries in taste (artists, DJs, experimental music communities) are the right early adopters for insect protein. They're already comfortable making the unconventional choice. Their audience watches them closely. If the DJ eats bugs, the crowd notices.

This is not influencer marketing. No one is paid to say they eat cricket powder. It's an ecosystem alignment: the people who earn from the protocol have an interest in the protocol's success, which is tied to the brand, which is tied to the powder.
